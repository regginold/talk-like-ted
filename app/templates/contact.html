About

I'm currently a graduate student at Washington University in St. Louis, where I'm studying how neural circuits regulate behavior. During my time here, I've developed several pieces of software to help biologists track the positions and postures of animals and use this information to make accurate predictions about their behavioral states. Along with an interest in using data to better understand behavior, I also enjoy making interesting, interactive, and user-friendly data visualizations.

During my free time, I'm often found outdoors rock climbing and hiking.
